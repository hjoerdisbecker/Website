  <div class="cv-bio">
    <p>
      Dr Hjördis Becker-Lindenthal is an Affiliated Researcher in the Philosophy of Religion
      at the Faculty of Divinity, University of Cambridge. Her work sits at the intersection
      of existential philosophy, mysticism, and theology, with a particular focus on
      Søren Kierkegaard's engagement with the medieval German mystic Johannes Tauler.
    </p>
    <p>
      She held a Leverhulme Early Career Fellowship at Cambridge (2018–2022), during which
      she developed her research project <em>Kierkegaard's Reception of Johannes Tauler</em>,
      and previously served as Research Associate to the Norris-Hulse Professor of Divinity,
      Prof. Sarah Coakley. Her doctoral dissertation — awarded <em>opus eximium</em> and
      the prize for the best doctoral dissertation in the Humanities at CAU Kiel (2015) —
      examined Kierkegaard's critique of culture and its philosophical consequences.
    </p>
    <p>
      She has held visiting fellowships at Harvard University and the Kierkegaard Research
      Centre, University of Copenhagen, and has presented her work at international
      conferences across Europe and North America. Her research engages questions of grace,
      selfhood, ecology, and mystical transformation, and has extended into public-facing
      work including BBC Radio, the Cambridge Festival of Ideas, and curatorial projects.
    </p>
