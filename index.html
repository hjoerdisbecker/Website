New Book

Kierkegaard and Mysticism

Reception, Influence, Resonance

This volume examines the relationship between Kierkegaard's thought and the Christian mystical tradition, tracing resonances, influences, and receptions across theology, philosophy, and spirituality. Edited by Hjördis Becker-Lindenthal. Routledge, 2025.

CV

Dr Hjördis Becker-Lindenthal is an Affiliated Researcher in the Philosophy of Religion at the Faculty of Divinity, University of Cambridge. Her work sits at the intersection of existential philosophy, mysticism, and theology, with a particular focus on Søren Kierkegaard's engagement with the medieval German mystic Johannes Tauler.

She held a Leverhulme Early Career Fellowship at Cambridge (2018–2022), during which she developed her research project Kierkegaard's Reception of Johannes Tauler, and previously served as Research Associate to the Norris-Hulse Professor of Divinity, Prof. Sarah Coakley. Her doctoral dissertation — awarded opus eximium and the prize for the best doctoral dissertation in the Humanities at CAU Kiel (2015) — examined Kierkegaard's critique of culture and its philosophical consequences.

She has held visiting fellowships at Harvard University and the Kierkegaard Research Centre, University of Copenhagen, and has presented her work at international conferences across Europe and North America. Her research engages questions of grace, selfhood, ecology, and mystical transformation, and has extended into public-facing work including BBC Radio, the Cambridge Festival of Ideas, and curatorial projects.
